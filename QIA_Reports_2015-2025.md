# Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) - Comprehensive Report 2015-2025

## Executive Summary

The Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) is Qatar's sovereign wealth fund, established in 2005 by Amiri Decision. As of August 2025, QIA manages approximately **$557 billion** in assets, ranking as the **world's eighth largest sovereign wealth fund**. This report compiles information about QIA's financial activities, investments, structure, and strategic developments from 2015 through 2025.

**Key Highlights:**
- Assets grew from ~$200 billion (2015) to $557 billion (2025)
- Active across 9 investment sectors globally
- Over 90% of portfolio concentrated in Qatar, Europe, and the U.S.
- Shifted away from hydrocarbon investments starting in 2020
- Notable transparency challenges but member of IFSWF

## Organizational Structure and Governance

### Establishment and Mandate
- **Founded:** 2005 by Amiri Decision
- **Mandate:** Strengthen Qatar's economy by diversifying into new asset classes
- **Governance:** Updated by Amiri Decision No. (34) in 2023

### Portfolio Structure
QIA's portfolio is split into two main components:
1. **Qatar Investments** - Large domestic holdings
2. **Qatar Holdings** - Overseas investments (formerly known as Qatar Holdings)

### Leadership Timeline (2015-2025)

**Chairmen:**
- **Sheikh Bandar bin Mohammed bin Saoud Al-Thani** (March 2023 - Present)

**Chief Executive Officers:**
- **Sheikh Abdullah bin Mohammed bin Saud Al Thani** (January 2015 - 2018)
  - Previously chairman of Qatari telecommunications company Ooredoo
- **Mansoor Ebrahim Al-Mahmoud** (July 2022 - November 2024)
- **Mohammed Saif Al-Sowaidi** (November 2024 - Present)

### Organizational Expansion
- **2015:** Opened office in New York City
- **2021:** Established office in Singapore to pursue Asian investment opportunities
- **2024:** Launched Active Asset Management Initiative with Ashmore Group as first partner

**Key Observations:**

- Continued growth from 2020 to 2025 (~$400B to $557B)
- From 2015 onwards, QIA embarked on a more controlled phase of diversification and stabilization
- As of 2021, more than half of assets invested in private equity and listed shares

## Transparency and Governance Framework

### Santiago Principles Compliance

**Background:**
- The Santiago Principles are 24 Generally Accepted Principles and Practices established in 2008
- Serve as globally accepted standards for governance, investment and risk management for sovereign wealth funds
- Designed to promote good governance, accountability, transparency and prudent investment practices

**QIA's Compliance:**
- Risk Management, Legal and Compliance departments review progress and compliance with Santiago Principles **annually**
- Member of International Forum of Sovereign Wealth Funds (IFSWF)

### Transparency Assessment

**Challenges:**
- **2014 Geoeconomica Assessment:** QIA placed in **"D" category** for Santiago compliance
- Primary demerits relate to transparency and documentation
- **Lacks detailed annual reports** - a significant black mark
- Structure and decision-making procedures characterized as **non-transparent**
- Does not publicly release detailed financial reports

**What QIA Does Disclose:**
- Complies with all public disclosures for **regulatory compliance purposes**
- Website shows some information about QIA's investments
- Performance reports and specific benchmarks remain **confidential**

**Comparison:**
- Similar transparency challenges to other Middle Eastern sovereign wealth funds
- Most Gulf region SWFs face similar transparency issues

## Conclusion

The Qatar Investment Authority has demonstrated significant growth and strategic evolution from 2015 to 2025:

**Financial Growth:**
- Nearly tripled AUM from ~$200B (2015) to $557B (2025)
- Maintained position among world's top 10 sovereign wealth funds

**Strategic Evolution:**
- Shifted from hydrocarbons to renewable energy and technology
- Expanded geographic presence with offices in NY and Singapore
- Increased focus on innovation sectors (AI, biotech, food tech)
- Maintained strong traditional holdings while diversifying

**Challenges:**
- Transparency remains limited compared to some global peers
- Continues to operate with significant confidentiality around performance metrics
- Gradual improvements in disclosure but still rated low on transparency scales

**Future Direction:**
- Major U.S. investment commitment ($500B over decade)
- Growing Asian market presence
- Technology and innovation focus
- Active asset management approach
- Renewable energy and sustainability emphasis
