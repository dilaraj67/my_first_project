# Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) - Comprehensive Report 2015-2025

## Executive Summary

The Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) is Qatar's sovereign wealth fund, established in 2005 by Amiri Decision [2][3]. As of August 2025, QIA manages approximately **$557 billion** in assets [2][5], ranking as the **world's eighth largest sovereign wealth fund** [2]. This report compiles information about QIA's financial activities, investments, structure, and strategic developments from 2015 through 2025.

**Key Highlights:**
- Assets grew from ~$200 billion (2015) to $557 billion (2025) [2]
- Active across 9 investment sectors globally [4][5]
- Over 90% of portfolio concentrated in Qatar, Europe, and the U.S. [14]
- Shifted away from hydrocarbon investments starting in 2020 [2]
- Notable transparency challenges but member of IFSWF [3][10]

## Organizational Structure and Governance

### Establishment and Mandate
- **Founded:** 2005 by Amiri Decision [2][3]
- **Mandate:** Strengthen Qatar's economy by diversifying into new asset classes [2]
- **Governance:** Updated by Amiri Decision No. (34) in 2023 [3]

### Portfolio Structure
QIA's portfolio is split into two main components [3]:
1. **Qatar Investments** - Large domestic holdings
2. **Qatar Holdings** - Overseas investments (formerly known as Qatar Holdings)

### Leadership Timeline (2015-2025)

**Chairmen:**
- **Sheikh Bandar bin Mohammed bin Saoud Al-Thani** (March 2023 - Present) [2]

**Chief Executive Officers:**
- **Sheikh Abdullah bin Mohammed bin Saud Al Thani** (January 2015 - 2018) [2]
  - Previously chairman of Qatari telecommunications company Ooredoo [2]
- **Mansoor Ebrahim Al-Mahmoud** (July 2022 - November 2024) [2]
- **Mohammed Saif Al-Sowaidi** (November 2024 - Present) [2]

### Organizational Expansion
- **2015:** Opened office in New York City [2]
- **2021:** Established office in Singapore to pursue Asian investment opportunities [14]
- **2024:** Launched Active Asset Management Initiative with Ashmore Group as first partner [5]

## Transparency and Governance Framework

### Santiago Principles Compliance

**Background:**
- The Santiago Principles are 24 Generally Accepted Principles and Practices established in 2008 [9][18]
- Serve as globally accepted standards for governance, investment and risk management for sovereign wealth funds [9][18]
- Designed to promote good governance, accountability, transparency and prudent investment practices [9][18]

**QIA's Compliance:**
- Risk Management, Legal and Compliance departments review progress and compliance with Santiago Principles **annually** [3][10]
- Member of International Forum of Sovereign Wealth Funds (IFSWF) [3][10]

### Transparency Assessment

**Challenges:**
- **2014 Geoeconomica Assessment:** QIA placed in **"D" category** for Santiago compliance [18]
- Primary demerits relate to transparency and documentation [18]
- **Lacks detailed annual reports** - a significant black mark [18]
- Structure and decision-making procedures characterized as **non-transparent** [2]
- Does not publicly release detailed financial reports [2]

**What QIA Does Disclose:**
- Complies with all public disclosures for **regulatory compliance purposes** [10]
- Website shows some information about QIA's investments [1][10]
- Performance reports and specific benchmarks remain **confidential** [10]

**Comparison:**
- Similar transparency challenges to other Middle Eastern sovereign wealth funds [18]
- Most Gulf region SWFs face similar transparency issues [18]

## Conclusion

The Qatar Investment Authority has demonstrated significant growth and strategic evolution from 2015 to 2025:

**Financial Growth:**
- Nearly tripled AUM from ~$200B (2015) to $557B (2025) [2][5]
- Maintained position among world's top 10 sovereign wealth funds [2][5]

**Strategic Evolution:**
- Shifted from hydrocarbons to renewable energy and technology [2][3][5]
- Expanded geographic presence with offices in NY and Singapore [2][14]
- Increased focus on innovation sectors (AI, biotech, food tech) [5][16]
- Maintained strong traditional holdings while diversifying [11][12]

**Challenges:**
- Transparency remains limited compared to some global peers [2][18]
- Continues to operate with significant confidentiality around performance metrics [10]
- Gradual improvements in disclosure but still rated low on transparency scales [18]

**Future Direction:**
- Major U.S. investment commitment ($500B over decade) [2]
- Growing Asian market presence [14]
- Technology and innovation focus [5][15]
- Active asset management approach [5]
- Renewable energy and sustainability emphasis [2][5]
