# WEEKLY JOB SEARCH SCHEDULE
## Sustainable 10-15 Hour/Week System

**Philosophy:** Consistency beats intensity. Small daily actions compound into job offers.

---

## WEEKLY STRUCTURE

### **MONDAY** (1.5 hours)
**Focus: Launch the week strong**

**LinkedIn Outreach (30 min):**
- Find 5 people to message
- Send 5 personalized messages
  - 2 HKS alumni
  - 2 IMF alumni
  - 1 cold/InMail
- Update outreach tracker

**Job Applications (45 min):**
- Apply to 3 jobs
- Prioritize companies where you have connections
- Update job tracker

**Admin (15 min):**
- Check for responses from last week
- Schedule any calls that came in
- Review week's goals

---

### **TUESDAY** (45 min)
**Focus: Follow-ups and relationship building**

**Response Management (20 min):**
- Reply to anyone who responded Monday
- Send thank you notes from any calls last week
- Propose times for new calls

**Research (25 min):**
- Deep dive on 2-3 companies you're targeting
- Find specific people at those companies
- Note recent company news/reports to reference

---

### **WEDNESDAY** (1.5 hours)
**Focus: Outreach momentum**

**LinkedIn Outreach (30 min):**
- Find 5 new people
- Send 5 messages
  - 2 at companies you just applied to
  - 2 at companies you're about to apply to
  - 1 senior person (MD/Partner)
- Update tracker

**Job Applications (45 min):**
- Apply to 2-3 jobs
- Tailor each application
- Update tracker

**Follow-Ups (15 min):**
- Send follow-up messages to people from last week who haven't responded

---

### **THURSDAY** (45 min)
**Focus: Informational interviews**

**Calls (30 min):**
- Take informational interviews
- OR prep for upcoming calls if none scheduled

**Networking Follow-Up (15 min):**
- Send thank you notes after calls
- Connect with anyone mentioned in conversations
- Update notes on what you learned

---

### **FRIDAY** (1 hour)
**Focus: Final push + weekly review**

**LinkedIn Outreach (20 min):**
- Send 3-5 final messages for the week
- Focus on high-priority targets

**Job Applications (20 min):**
- Apply to 1-2 more jobs
- Reach goal of 5-10 applications/week

**Weekly Review (20 min):**
- Update all trackers
- Calculate response rate
- Plan next week's targets
- Celebrate wins (responses, calls, interviews!)

---

### **WEEKEND** (2-3 hours total)
**Focus: Deep work and preparation**

**Saturday (1-1.5 hours):**
- Prep for any Monday/Tuesday calls
- Research companies deeply (not just LinkedIn)
- Update resume/CV if needed
- Write custom cover letters for high-priority apps

**Sunday (1-1.5 hours):**
- Review company targets list
- Plan Monday's outreach targets
- Batch research: Find 10-15 people to message next week
- Mental prep for the week

**OPTIONAL Sunday Evening:**
- Review templates
- Update LinkedIn profile if needed
- Read industry news to stay current

---

## DAILY MINIMUMS (If You're Busy)

Even on crazy days, do this:

**10-Minute Minimum:**
- Send 1 LinkedIn message
- Check for responses
- Update tracker

**The compound effect:** 1 message/day = 7/week = 30/month = 10-12 responses = 5-6 calls = job opportunities

## TIME BLOCKING TIPS

**Best times to send LinkedIn messages:**
- Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am (highest response rate)
- Avoid Monday early AM and Friday afternoon

**Best times to schedule calls:**
- Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-3pm
- Avoid Monday mornings (people are catching up)

**Best times to apply for jobs:**
- Early in the week (Monday-Tuesday)
- Applications reviewed sooner

---

## ENERGY MANAGEMENT

**High Energy Needed:**
- Writing new messages
- Informational interviews
- Job applications

**Medium Energy:**
- Follow-ups
- Research
- Tracker updates

**Low Energy:**
- Responding to messages
- Scheduling calls
- Reading company news

**Match tasks to your energy level throughout the day.**

- Monday: 3 messages + 2 applications (45 min)
- Wednesday: 3 messages + 2 applications (45 min)

## ADJUSTMENT POINTS

**If response rate is low (<20%):**
- Review message templates
- Add more personalization
- Try different connection types

**If not getting interviews from applications:**
- Message someone at the company before/after applying
- Improve resume/cover letter
- Apply to more realistic targets

**If calls aren't converting to referrals:**
- Ask for intros more directly
- Follow up better after calls
- Provide more value in conversations

**Stay flexible. Adjust based on what's working.**
